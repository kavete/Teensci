Human Eye Defects and Problems

Defects of the Human Eye

There are four major human eye defects. They include Myopia/ Shortsightedness/ Nearsightedness, Hyperopia/ Farsightedness, Presbyopia and Astigmatism.

Myopia or Nearsightedness or Shortsightedness

When one has this defect, objects that are far appear blurred but near objects are seen clearly.
This is because the Image is focused in front of the retina rather than on the retina itself (normally images are formed on the retina).

Causes of myopia

  1. ✔Long Eyeball
  2. ✔Strong refractive power of the lens

Myopia can be corrected by use of concave lenses to diverge the light rays to the retina.

Hyperopia or Farsightedness

For the case of Hyperopia, there is difficulty with near vision (near objects) but no problems with far objects vision.
This is because instead of the image being formed on the retina it is focused behind the retina.

Causes of hyperopia

  1. ✔Short Eyeball
  2. ✔Weak refractive power of the lens

Farsightedness can be corrected by using a convex lens to converge the light rays on the retina.

Astigmatism

Astigmatism causes vision to be blurred or distorted to some degree at all distances. Symptoms of Astigmatism can be be eye strain and headaches.
This defect is due to light rays not coming to a single focal point on the retina. Some of the light rays are focused on the retina, others are formed behind and others infront of the retina.

This defect is caused by non-uniform curvature of the cornea
Astigmatism can be corrected by use of special cylindrical lenses.

Presbyopia

This eye defect makes one experience blurred near vision when reading, sewing or working with a computer. Everyone becomes presbyopic as they age.

Problems of the eye

Glaucoma

Glaucoma is caused by increased intraocular pressure. Intraocular pressure (IOP) is the balance between the production and drainage of aqueous humor. It can also cause permanet loss of vision.

Cataracts

Cataracts is the clouding of the lens that prevents a clear and sharp image being produced causing blurred images.

ARMD(Age Related Macular Degeneration)

This is caused by hardening of the arteries that nourish the retina. In other words, it is the degenerative condition of the macula (The Central Retina).
